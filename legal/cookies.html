Cookie Policy

Last updated: February 16, 2026

1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

Cookies can be "persistent" or "session" cookies:

  • Persistent cookies: Remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them
  • Session cookies: Temporary cookies that are deleted when you close your browser

2. How We Use Cookies

Home Recipe Journal uses cookies to:

  • Remember your cookie consent preferences
  • Enhance your browsing experience
  • Understand how you use our website
  • Improve our website functionality
  • Analyze website traffic and user behavior

3. Types of Cookies We Use

Essential Cookies (Always Active)

These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. They enable basic features like:

  • Cookie consent preferences (remembering if you accepted or declined cookies)
  • Security features
  • Basic website functionality

Cookie Name: cookieConsent
Purpose: Stores your cookie consent choice
Duration: Persistent (no expiration unless you clear browser data)
Provider: Home Recipe Journal (first-party)

Analytics Cookies (Optional)

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously:

  • Pages visited
  • Time spent on pages
  • Links clicked
  • Browser and device type
  • Geographic location (country/city level)

We may use third-party analytics services such as Google Analytics. These services use their own cookies.

Preference Cookies (Optional)

These cookies remember your preferences and settings to provide a more personalized experience:

  • Language preferences
  • Display settings
  • Newsletter subscription status

4. Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies on our website are placed by third-party services we use:

Google Analytics: We may use Google Analytics to understand how visitors use our site. Google Analytics uses cookies to collect information about your use of the website and create reports for us. The information includes your IP address, browser type, and pages visited. For more information, see Google's Privacy Policy.

Social Media Plugins: If we include social media sharing buttons, these platforms may set cookies when you interact with them.

5. Managing Cookies

Cookie Consent Banner

When you first visit our website, you'll see a cookie consent banner. You can:

  • Accept all cookies
  • Decline optional cookies (only essential cookies will be used)

Your choice is stored in the "cookieConsent" cookie and will be remembered for future visits.

Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • View what cookies are stored
  • Delete cookies
  • Block cookies from specific websites
  • Block all cookies

Here's how to manage cookies in popular browsers:

  • Chrome: Settings > Privacy and Security > Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Settings > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Manage Website Data
  • Edge: Settings > Privacy, search, and services > Cookies and site data

Note: Blocking all cookies may affect your experience on our website and prevent certain features from working properly.

Opt-Out of Analytics

To opt out of Google Analytics across all websites, you can install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

6. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" feature that lets you tell websites you don't want your online activities tracked. Our website does not currently respond to Do Not Track signals, but you can manage cookies through your browser settings as described above.

7. Mobile Devices

If you access our website through a mobile device, you can manage cookies through your device settings. The process varies by device and operating system.
